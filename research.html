Master's Thesis – Applied Economics

Topic: Crop Diversification and Climate Change Adaptation in Low and Middle-Income Countries

My current research explores crop diversification as a strategy for climate change adaptation in low and middle-income countries. I apply econometric methods to analyze how diversifying crop choices can help farmers reduce vulnerability to climate variability and improve resilience.

This thesis combines economic theory, policy analysis, and data-driven econometric modeling to contribute to the growing field of sustainable agriculture and development economics.
